What is intelligent automation?

Imagine all the apps and software you work with are people. None of those people speak the same language, so historically, the workforce has consisted of humans who learned each of the languages in order to give directions to the apps.

Intelligent Automation introduces a translator known as an  Application Programming Interface (API) so the apps can speak directly to each other, and AI to direct what they do with the information they pass. The AI operates on a set of directives very much like a person would, and can greatly reduce the need for human intervention in business processes.

Some use cases:

  • Customer Support: AI-driven chatbots and ticketing systems handle routine inquiries, escalate complex issues, and track resolution times, improving efficiency and customer satisfaction.
  • Booking and Reservation Systems: Fully automated systems handle reservations, cancellations, and even upselling, creating a seamless experience for customers and reducing workload for staff.
  • Lead Generation: Automatically capture, qualify, and assign leads to your sales team, ensuring no opportunity slips through the cracks. Think of it as a digital sales assistant working 24/7.
  • Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Automation: Automate follow-ups, reminders, and client updates directly from your CRM, keeping your sales pipeline moving without manual effort.
  • Data Analysis and Reporting: Automate the collection and analysis of key metrics, delivering real-time dashboards and reports that help you make faster, smarter decisions.
  • Inventory Management: Automate stock tracking, reorder points, and supplier communication. The system predicts demand, prevents overstocking, and ensures you’re always ready to meet customer needs.
  • Resource Planning and Allocation: Automate scheduling and resource allocation for projects or staff, ensuring optimal use of time and assets while avoiding bottlenecks.
  • Vendor and Supply Chain Management: Automate purchase orders, track shipments, and monitor supplier performance, creating a streamlined supply chain that reduces delays and errors.
  • Workflow Automation: Integrate and automate processes across departments—like sales, finance, and operations—so tasks like approvals, invoicing, and reporting happen seamlessly.
  • Social Media Management: Schedule posts, track engagement, and respond to inquiries automatically, ensuring your brand stays active and responsive without constant oversight.
